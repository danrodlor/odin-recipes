Orellas

Description

Orellas is a traditional galician dish. Orellas is a galician word that basically means ears. It is typically prepared during carnival time and Lent.

As you may have suspected, this dish is called orellas because it resembles a mountain of cooked pig ears. This is not a coincidence, the fact this dish is traditionally prepared during carnival time and Lent serves two purposes: to take advantage of the last days before the Lent fast, and to mimic a good ration of pig ears to trick the brain without breaking the Lent meat consumption restrictions.

Along with the filloas and the caldo, the orellas constitute the typical galician carnival menu.

Despite its simplicity and humble origins, its flavour will captivate you!

Ingredients

The amounts are given for 24 units.

  • Wheat flour - 500g
  • Small sized eggs - 2 units
  • Water - 200ml
  • Sweet anise liqueur - 50ml
  • Lard - 100g
  • sugar - 75g
  • Lemon/orange zest - 1 unit
  • Salt - Just a pinch
  • Powdered sugar - To taste
  • Powdered cinnamon - To taste
  • Sunflower oil - To fry

Steps

  1. First things first; wash your hands!
  2. Put the water in a big bowl and add the melted lard, the zest, the sugar, the sweet anise liqueur, the eggs and the salt. Mix it well until it becomes homogeneous.
  3. Add the flour little by little at the same time you stir the mixture to avoid lumps. Once it takes some consistency, leave the rods aside and use your hands. Beat it until you get a greasy and nice dough.
  4. Let the dough rest for at least for an hour.
  5. Now it is time to extend the dough and give it shape! Take small portions of the dough (approximately of the size of a walnut) and work them out with the help of a rolling pin. Remember, you have to make the orellas as thin as you can. Once they have the desired thickness, fold one side to give them the shape of a pig ear.
  6. Put a deep frying pan or a small saucepan with a generous amount of sunflower oil at medium heat. Throw in a small dough ball. This ball will act as a tester, once it turns golden, it is time to start frying our orellas, side by side.
  7. Remove them from the pan and let them rest on absorbent paper to avoid excess oil.
  8. Once all the orellas are ready, sprinkle a mix of powdered sugar and cinnamon over them!
