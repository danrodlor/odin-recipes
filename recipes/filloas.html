Filloas

Description

Filloas is a traditional galician dish. It is typically prepared during Autumn and Winter, starting just after the pig slaughter, and carnival time.

The origins of this dish go back to the years of the Roman empire and its phyllon, word which has greek origins and means literally "sheet". The phyllon was elaborated with water, eggs, flour, honey and spices. This dish is the precursor of several similar dishes that spread across different european countries (specifically those which cultivated cereals). There are a lot of varieties of filloas! Depending on the time of the year and the geography, some of its ingredients can change, as well as the way it is consumed (it can be a starter, a side or even a dessert!).

Despite its simplicity and humble origins, its flavour will captivate you!

Ingredients

The amounts are given for 30 units.

  • Wheat flour - 200g
  • Medium sized eggs - 3 units
  • Milk - 500ml
  • Sweet anise liqueur - 30ml
  • Honey - As much as you want!
  • Salt - Just a pinch
  • Fresh pork belly - A fat slice

Steps

  1. First things first; wash your hands!
  2. Mix the flour, the eggs, the milk, the sweet anise liqueur and the salt all together in a bowl. Stir until the mixture becomes homogeneous.
  3. Once the mix is ready, heat up a pan (or a filloeira if you have one!) and rub the fresh pork belly over its surface until it is well greased.
  4. Now, it is time to make your first filloa! Grab a coffee cup and fill it with our mixture. Then, just pour its content over the pan at the same time you move it trying to cover all the bottom with the liquid.
  5. Wait until you can see little bubbles arising out of the middle of our filloa and then flip it over and continue cooking the other side for 30 seconds more. Next, remove it from the pan and put it on a plate.
  6. Repeat the steps 3 to 5 until you ran out of mixture! Your filloas stack will grow quickly.
  7. Finally, put some honey on the first filloa of the stack, wrap it and let your tongue taste it!
